Why this exists

A few months ago, I was invited to a show at a gallery in a building I'd walked past countless times over the years — and never knew there was a gallery inside.

That got me wondering: how many other galleries had I walked past without ever knowing? How much art was I missing, just because a space doesn't have a storefront sign? Everyone knows the obvious names — the big Chelsea spaces, the major museums — but it turns out New York has a lot of smaller, quieter galleries tucked into buildings across all five boroughs that most people, even longtime residents, never notice.

So I built this: an interactive map of nearly 300 NYC galleries, checked weekly for new shows, so you can see what's opening near you — including the hidden ones you've probably walked past too. A weekly email will let you know when something new shows up, if you don't want to check the map yourself.

This isn't affiliated with any gallery, museum, or arts organization. It's just something I built because I got curious about how much good art was hiding in plain sight around this city.
